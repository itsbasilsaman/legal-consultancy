Frequently asked questions

Expert legal solutions tailored to Saudi Arabian laws and regulations
What are the key banking regulations in Saudi Arabia?

The Saudi banking sector is primarily regulated by the Saudi Central Bank (SAMA) under the Banking Control Law. Key regulations include capital adequacy requirements, anti-money laundering rules, and Sharia compliance for Islamic banks. We stay updated with all regulatory changes to ensure our clients remain compliant with evolving Saudi financial regulations.

How does Islamic finance differ from conventional banking?

Islamic finance prohibits interest (riba) and instead operates on profit-sharing models like Murabaha, Ijara, and Musharaka. All transactions must be asset-backed and comply with Sharia principles. Our legal team specializes in structuring Sharia-compliant financial products that meet both religious requirements and business objectives.

What legal support do you offer for fintech companies?

We provide comprehensive legal services for fintech companies including regulatory compliance with SAMA's fintech sandbox, licensing applications, data privacy compliance under Saudi data protection laws, and drafting fintech agreements. Our team understands the unique challenges fintech companies face in the Saudi market.

How can foreign banks establish operations in Saudi Arabia?

Foreign banks can establish branches or subsidiaries in Saudi Arabia by obtaining a license from SAMA. The process involves meeting capital requirements, demonstrating financial stability, and complying with Saudi ownership rules. We guide foreign banks through the entire licensing process and ongoing compliance requirements.

What are the legal requirements for anti-money laundering in Saudi banks?

Saudi banks must comply with the Anti-Money Laundering Law and its implementing regulations, which include customer due diligence, transaction monitoring, reporting suspicious activities, and staff training. We help banks develop robust AML compliance programs tailored to Saudi regulations and international standards.
